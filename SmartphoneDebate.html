Smartphone Debate.... Do Smartphones Belong in the Classroom?



so....the debate comes again



Ask any 21st century student and they will probably be on their phone while they are talking to you.
Many schools already have BYOD computers or iPads but phones are still a big NO. If you can use your personal
laptop or iPad, then why can't students use their phones? Your phone can do the exact same thing as
your iPad and laptop (nearly) but just a smaller version. so why can't they be used in class then?





Pros:


Phones are light and easy to carry around and use for

quick access to web resources and documents

-----------------------------------------------------

You can easily use your phone to take photos or

record notes easily if you are outside or doing a

science experiment

-----------------------------------------------------

You can make use of mobile learning by being able

view docs, images and resources without needing to

carry a large device to school

-----------------------------------------------------

Students can easily share things they are doing via

social media which can also be good VTC for the school

Cons:


Phones can cause distractions for students

when they are meant to be listening in class

or working

-----------------------------------------------------

Students could use phones to cheat in exams or

or copy answers of the web

-----------------------------------------------------

Some students have trouble concentrating if they

have their device on the table or in their pockets

-----------------------------------------------------

Students might lose social skills or ignore other

people as they are used to being on their devices so

often
